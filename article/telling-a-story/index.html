Telling a story

We’re good at doing this at cxpartners. Taking existing research findings, our knowledge of each sector or pulling information from an existing customer experience map. We can help tell the story behind the innovation.

  1. Pain points (from user testing or a cxMap)
  2. Competitor benchmarking (eg through a SWOT)
  3. Signature moments i.e. brand resonance / alignment (from user testing or a cxMap)
  4. Gaps (from the cxMap)

An example from Appliances Online

Let’s look at an example. Appliances Online sell electrical items online. We have done user research for them before and we’ve produced a customer experience map. One of the biggest gaps was spotted was customers buying a full set of appliances when they move house. Currently 75% of AO.com’s sales are single appliances. We saw many users struggling to find matching appliances for a new kitchen. The idea for this innovation. Find matching appliances for a new kitchen.

Another example from Experian

Will spotted an opportunity at Experian from their customer experience map following a competitor benchmarking research project. Experian don’t have a free version of their Credit Expert product but an upstart competitor is stealing customers as they do have a free offering. cx and Experian then spent a few weeks designing Experian Free.

How we do it

As with all parts of the cxInnovation product we split our approach into three sizes depending on time and budget.

Small

  • We’ll help you gather the intel.
  • We’ll run a design & innovation workshop to develop the idea and the story around it.

Medium

  • We’ll run a design & innovation workshop to develop the idea and the story around it.
  • We’ll run a business workshops to develop, refine and plan the approach.

Large

  • We’ll run research to uncover the next innovation in your industry. Insight bank.
  • We’ll run a design & innovation workshop to develop the idea and the story around it.
