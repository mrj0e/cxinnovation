Brainstorming ideas

We have the opportunity statement, now we need generate ideas from it.

There are lots of issues with the typical brainstorming approach. Loud voices and the chance that ideas generated early on take the focus away from better ideas generated later (ref).

The best way to approach the problem of idea generation is to spend some time upfront writing ideas down. Called Brainwriting it generates loads more ideas that brainstorming and helps shut the loud mouth up.

How does it work

Brainwriting is simple. Rather than ask participants to shout out ideas (a serial process), you ask them to write down their ideas about a particular question or problem on sheets of paper for a few minutes. Next you have each participant pass their ideas on to someone else, who reads the ideas and adds new ideas.

After a few minutes, you ask the participants to pass their papers to others, and the process repeats. After 10 to 15 minutes, you collect the sheets and post them for immediate discussion.
