Getting the go-ahead

To take the project to launch we need to ask the board for the money to build it.

By this point we have the building blocks for the pitch.

We need to do two thing to ensure we get the go ahead, speak to the right people ahead of time and prepare the pitch deck.

Speak to the right people ahead of time.

It’s important to know the people you will be pitching to for funds for the project.

Approaching them ahead of time, for example asking them to complete the idea scorecard so they get early input as to what ideas may work.

Running through the various parts of the business plan with them ahead of time will allow you to understand what concerns they have and address them ahead of the pitch itself.

Adobe Kickbox has some good advice on speaking to direct reports for each director to get the inside track.

Pitch perfectly

The pitch presentation you are using needs to be short and succinct. You don’t need the supporting information but you will need to know it when questions come up.

Ideally you want the presentation to last 10 minutes and have a further 20 minutes for questions.
