Research

We can run some research to uncover what problems users are having within a current market.

We are pretty familiar with how this works at cxpartners.

A example might be car insurance. We can speak to 12 respondents and understand what challenges they find with car insurance. Be it getting insurance, renewing or claiming, we can focus in one area where user needs may not be being met.

The biggest challenge with this kind of research is we don’t know what we don’t know. We don’t know what kinds of thing we will find out but we will find something worth pursuing.

It needs to be lean

The research itself can be involved but the reporting and feedback loop needs to be removed.

The client should be involved throughout the research and can help us spot opportunities. There won’t be a big final presentation with a ta-da message.

Ideas will come up be recorded throughout the research and we’ll discuss them as we go.

It may not be in a lab

The best kind of investigatory research happens away from the research lab.

Ethnographic research in users’ homes or workplaces will highlight the wider context of what’s happening and allow for richer data on which to base ideas.
