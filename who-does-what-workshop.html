Who does what and when workshop

A workshop where we decide who is responsible for each part of the project.

What we are doing and who will do it

Preparation: To prepare for the workshop you will need to write each project element on an index card. Here’s one you can modify and print (there’s a guillotine in the drawer under the printer and Lou and Tash have a glue stick). You will need to prepare a large sheet of brown paper with the next 3 months on it. Each week should be the width of one index card 5" or 13cm.

Each step of the project is written on an index card (or post-it).

Example:

Writing the business case - Costs, profit projections, break even and financing - Inputs / dependencies: analytics - Responsible: Jane Smith [client] Finance Director - Support: Sally from cxpartners

These are then placed on a time planning wall sheet.

Key questions

We want to know who is ultimately in charge and who holds the purse strings. These people will ensure the success of the project.

  • Who is the project sponsor?
  • What is the process for sign-off for funds for this investigation project and ultimately for the large project?
  • Who has the budget for the project? Are there other managers who could co-fund the project?
  • What do we have to do to get the budget?

Stakeholder management

We need to know who is involved in this project and what their needs are. We expect to identify 5 - 10 stakeholders here. We expect to identify further stakeholders once we begin the interviews so we’ll add 3 extra / spare slots. The interviews will be face to face or over the phone.

  • Who are the immediate stakeholders?
  • Who are the wider team?
  • Who are the decision makers?
  • Who should we speak to? Let’s make a list.
  • cxpartners will write the invitation blurb (See stakeholder interviews for more detail)
  • Are there any other projects that support or conflict with this project?
